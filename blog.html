Building a post-operative recovery companion that stays with the patient after discharge

Vision Beyond the OR is my achievement from a healthcare hackathon: a recovery-support platform that helps patients complete daily check-ins, share symptoms in natural language, track medication adherence, and surface escalation signals when recovery moves off track.

End-to-end Patient check-in, AI response, and provider summary flow
Safety first Supportive guidance only, with deterministic escalation rules
Voice-ready Designed for low-friction, mobile-friendly recovery updates
Trackable Structured recovery signals for trend review over time
What makes it meaningful

It turns post-op recovery into a guided experience instead of a one-time discharge handoff, while keeping company-specific details masked for public sharing.

The problem I set out to solve

After surgery, patients often go home with instructions, medication schedules, and a long gap before the next follow-up. That leaves room for uncertainty, missed drops, and delayed escalation when symptoms are changing.

The goal of this project was to close that gap with a simple daily recovery companion that feels supportive for patients and useful for clinicians.

What I built

Patient check-in experience

  • Daily symptom capture in plain language
  • Medication adherence tracking
  • Mobile-friendly flow for quick use at home

AI-assisted recovery support

  • Supportive responses based on the patient report
  • Structured extraction of recovery signals
  • Clear boundary: not a diagnosis engine

Provider-facing summary

  • Recovery classification: expected, monitor, or escalate
  • Short summary of reasons and trends
  • Built for fast review instead of noise

How the system works

1
Patient completes a check-in The flow collects symptoms, medication adherence, and optional recovery inputs.
2
The input is interpreted The system converts free text into a structured recovery snapshot.
3
Rules decide the next action Deterministic logic marks the case as expected, monitor, or escalate.
4
Provider summary is prepared If needed, the dashboard gets a concise summary for follow-up review.

Why this project matters to me

I wanted to build something that is both practical and humane: practical because it helps organize follow-up care, and humane because it gives patients a clearer sense of support when they are recovering at home.

The most important part of the build was keeping the experience safe and understandable. It is designed to support recovery conversations, not replace clinical judgment.
